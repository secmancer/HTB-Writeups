- Information security (infosec) is a broad and evolving field with many specializations, including:
    - Network and infrastructure security
    - Application security
    - Security testing
    - Systems auditing
    - Business continuity planning
    - Digital forensics
    - Incident detection and response
- Infosec involves protecting data from unauthorized access, alteration, misuse, or disruption.
- Professionals aim to minimize the impact of security incidents.
- Data can be electronic or physical, and either tangible (e.g., blueprints) or intangible (e.g., knowledge).
- The core principle of infosec is maintaining the **CIA triad** — **Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability** of data.


# Risk Management Process
- Data protection policies must balance **effectiveness and efficiency** without harming business operations.
- Organizations use the **risk management process** to achieve this balance, which includes five steps:
    1. **Identifying the Risk:** Determine potential risks (legal, environmental, market, regulatory, etc.).
    2. **Analyze the Risk:** Assess each risk’s impact and likelihood; align with policies and procedures.
    3. **Evaluate the Risk:** Rank and prioritize risks; choose to accept, avoid, control, or transfer them.        
    4. **Dealing with Risk:** Mitigate or eliminate risks through collaboration with relevant stakeholders.
    5. **Monitoring Risk:** Continuously track risks and update their impact levels as situations change.
- The core goal of infosec is **information assurance**, maintaining the **Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability (CIA)** of data during incidents such as natural disasters, malfunctions, or attacks.


# Red Team vs. Blue Team
- In infosec, **red teams** and **blue teams** represent attackers and defenders, respectively.
- **Red Team:**
    - Acts as adversaries to find weaknesses in the organization’s defenses.
    - Performs tasks like **penetration testing**, **social engineering**, and other offensive techniques.
- **Blue Team:**
    - Focuses on **defense and protection** of the organization’s systems.
    - Handles **risk analysis**, **policy creation**, **threat detection**, **incident response**, and use of **security tools**.
    - Represents the majority of infosec roles.


# Role of Penetration Testers
- **Security assessor role:** identifies risks in networks and applications, including vulnerabilities, misconfigurations, data exposure, and potential reputational damage.
- **Tester responsibilities:** help clients recognize risks, demonstrate how to reproduce them, and advise on mitigation or remediation.
- **Types of assessments:**
    - **White-box testing:** full-scope vulnerability identification.
    - **Phishing assessments:** test employee awareness.
    - **Red team exercises:** simulate real-world attacker scenarios.
- **Context awareness:** understanding the organization’s environment is essential for accurate risk evaluation.
- **Risk management:** fundamental for beginners in information security.
- **Module focus:**
    - Choosing and using a penetration testing distro.
    - Learning core technologies and tools.
    - Understanding penetration testing levels and basics.
    - Completing a first Hack The Box (HTB) machine.
    - Learning how to seek help effectively and avoid common issues.
    - Navigating the HTB platform.
- **Applicability:** although HTB and intentionally vulnerable systems are used, the skills apply to any real-world environment.
