- **Threat Actor Team:**
    - An **organized group** of individuals with **specialized skills** working together to conduct **cyberattacks**.
    - Operate with **malicious intent**, aiming to **breach defenses**, **steal data**, **cause disruption**, or **gain financial or political advantage**.
- **Comparison with Security Teams:**
    - **Red Teams:** Use similar offensive tactics but with the goal of **testing and improving** an organization’s defenses.
    - **Blue Teams:** Defend systems by **detecting, preventing, and responding** to attacks.
    - Threat actor teams differ in that their **objective is harm or exploitation**, not protection or learning.

- **Threat actor team composition:** organized groups with specialized roles working together to carry out cyber attacks.
- **Common team roles:**
    - **Expert Programmers:** develop custom malware and exploit code.
    - **Network Specialists:** map and navigate target infrastructures, find weak points.
    - **Social Engineers:** manipulate people to obtain credentials or access.
    - **Data Analysts / Exfiltration Specialists:** process and extract valuable stolen data while hiding traces.
    - **Team Leaders / Strategists:** coordinate operations, set objectives, and plan tactics.
- **Individual actors:** “lone wolves” can operate alone, using varied skill sets from simple phishing to advanced malware development.
- **Heist analogy mapping:**
    - **Scout → Reconnaissance (OSINT, scanners, social probing).**
    - **Lockpicker → Exploitation (malware, rootkits, custom scripts).**
    - **Getaway driver → Exfiltration (encrypted channels, VPNs, obfuscation).**
    - **Leader → Coordination and operational planning.**
- **Tools & techniques used:** network scanners, OSINT tooling, malware, custom scripts, encrypted comms, VPNs, data obfuscation, credential harvesting, phishing, supply-chain manipulation.
- **Operational tradecraft:** prefer **“low and slow”** stealth techniques to avoid detection; avoid noisy methods (e.g., obvious brute force or detectable malware).    
- **Key risk:** specialized, coordinated teams (or skilled individuals) can execute sophisticated, persistent, and hard-to-detect operations with high impact.


# Objectives
- **Primary Objective:** Threat actors aim to **infiltrate and exploit** target systems or networks for various purposes.
- ### **Common Motivations**
	1. **Financial Gain:**
	    - Theft of money through **fraudulent transactions** or **ransomware**.
	    - Selling stolen data, credentials, or trade secrets on the **dark web**.
	2. **Espionage:**
	    - Gathering **confidential information** from corporations or governments.
	    - Used for **strategic, economic, or political advantage**.
	3. **Disruption:**
	    - Intentionally **causing chaos** by disabling systems, deleting data, or spreading misinformation.
	    - Common in **hacktivism** or **state-sponsored sabotage**.
	4. **Ideological Motives:**
	    - Driven by **political, religious, or social beliefs**.
	    - Targets organizations that **oppose their ideology** (e.g., hacktivist groups).
	5. **Revenge:**
	    - Attacks motivated by **personal grievances or retaliation** against individuals or organizations.
	- **Key Insight:**	    
	    - Threat actors are **diverse in motive and method**, making cyber threats **multifaceted and unpredictable** across today’s global digital landscape.
