- **Disaster Recovery (DR)** and **Business Continuity (BC)** are key elements of an organization’s **resilience strategy**, ensuring operations continue during major disruptions.
- Though often linked, they serve **different purposes**:
    - **Disaster Recovery (DR):** Focuses on restoring **critical systems and data** after a catastrophic event.
- **Types of events:**
    - **Natural disasters:** Earthquakes, floods, storms.
    - **Man-made incidents:** Fires, terrorism, or sabotage.
    - **Technological failures:** System crashes, hardware failures, or cyberattacks.
- **Goal of DR:** Minimize **downtime** and **data loss**, enabling rapid restoration of essential business functions.
- **Typical DR plan components:**
    - **Data backup procedures** to ensure recoverability.
    - **System replication** for redundancy.
    - **Failover mechanisms** to switch operations to alternate sites or cloud environments.

### **Disaster Recovery (DR)**
- Like **bringing an umbrella and a generator** to keep the show running during rain or power failure.
- Focuses on **restoring critical systems and data** after an incident (e.g., cyberattack, natural disaster).
- Aims to **minimize downtime** and get essential functions back online quickly.
- Involves technical recovery measures such as **system restoration, backups,** and **failover procedures.**


### **Business Continuity (BC)**
- A **broader, proactive plan** for maintaining operations during and after disruptions.
- Like **moving the concert indoors** or switching to an **acoustic performance** if major systems fail.
- Ensures the business continues to function—possibly in an adjusted form—through strategies like:
    - **Remote work** options.
    - **Alternative suppliers or locations.**
    - **Flexible operational procedures.**


### **Key Takeaways**
- **DR** = Technical recovery of critical systems after disruption.
- **BC** = Organizational strategy to keep operations running during and after crises.
- Together, they:
    - Reduce **financial losses** and **downtime**.
    - Maintain **customer trust** and **regulatory compliance**.
    - Protect the organization’s **reputation** and long-term viability.


# Responsibility
- **Responsibility for DR and BC:**
    - Managed by a **dedicated Business Continuity or Resilience team**, typically led by a **Business Continuity Manager** or equivalent.
    - This team collaborates with **IT, operations, and executive leadership** to design, implement, and maintain DR/BC plans.
- **Key Responsibilities:**
    - Conduct **risk assessments** to identify potential threats.
    - Determine **critical business functions** that must be restored first.
    - Define **Recovery Time Objectives (RTOs)** — how quickly systems must be restored.
    - Define **Recovery Point Objectives (RPOs)** — how much data loss is acceptable.
    - Develop and maintain strategies to meet these recovery goals.
- **Penetration Testers’ Role:**
    - Help **identify vulnerabilities** that could undermine DR/BC effectiveness.
    - Test the **resilience of recovery procedures** and supporting systems.
- **Testing DR/BC Plans:**
    - Essential for ensuring effectiveness and staff readiness.
    - **Types of tests:**
        - **Tabletop exercises:** Walk-through discussions of response actions.
        - **Full-scale simulations:** Live failover to backup systems or alternate sites.
    - **Frequency:** Typically **annual**, but may vary based on organization size, complexity, and regulatory requirements.
- **Goal:** Maintain a well-tested, reliable recovery strategy that ensures **business continuity, data integrity, and operational resilience** during crises.
