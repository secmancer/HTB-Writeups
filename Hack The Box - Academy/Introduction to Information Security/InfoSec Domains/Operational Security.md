- **Operational Security (OpSec)** is a key part of an organization’s overall security framework.
- It focuses on the **processes, practices, and decisions** that protect data assets throughout their lifecycle.
- The main objective is to ensure a **secure operational environment** for daily business activities.
- OpSec safeguards sensitive information by maintaining its:
    - **Confidentiality** – restricting access to authorized personnel only.
    - **Integrity** – ensuring data remains accurate and unaltered.
    - **Availability** – keeping information accessible to those who need it.

- **Operational Security (OpSec)** is like preparing for a big party — it’s about protecting what matters most while allowing things to run smoothly.


### **1. Asset Identification**
- Identify what’s most valuable and needs protection — the **critical information assets**.
- In a party analogy: deciding which items (e.g., jewelry, game console) require extra care.


### **2. Threat Identification**
- Analyze potential risks or things that could go wrong.
- Example: someone knocking over the console or entering a private room.
- In OpSec: assessing **threats** and **vulnerabilities** that could expose sensitive data.


### **3. Vulnerability Identification**
- Identify weak points and take preventive action.
- Example: locking valuables away or restricting areas.
- In OpSec: using **passwords**, **security badges**, and **surveillance** to safeguard assets.


### **4. Access Control**
- Determine **who can access what** and under what conditions.
- Example: only trusted friends have a key to your room.
- In OpSec: implement **authentication** (e.g., MFA) and **authorization** controls to limit data access.
- Conduct **regular audits** to revoke access when no longer needed.


### **5. Monitoring**
- Continuously watch for suspicious behavior and adjust defenses.
- Example: ensuring guests stay in allowed areas during the party.    
- In OpSec: **continuous monitoring** helps detect and respond to new or changing threats.


### **Key Supporting Components**
- **Asset Management:**
    - Maintain an up-to-date **inventory** of all hardware, software, and data.
    - Know what assets exist, where they’re located, and their importance.
- **Change Management:**
    - Implement system or process changes **safely and systematically**.
    - Ensure all updates are tested and approved to prevent new vulnerabilities.
- **Security Awareness Training:**
    - Educate employees about **phishing**, **strong passwords**, and **safe data handling**.
    - Reinforce that **security is everyone’s responsibility**.


### **Summary**
- OpSec is a **continuous and adaptive process** focused on:
    - Identifying critical assets.
    - Recognizing and mitigating threats.
    - Managing vulnerabilities.
    - Controlling and monitoring access.
    - Promoting security awareness across the organization.
- Its ultimate goal: keep operations secure while maintaining confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information.


# OpSec Responsibility
- **Responsibility for OpSec:**
    - Primarily handled by the **Information Security team**, led by the **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** or an equivalent role.
    - The CISO collaborates with **IT, HR, Legal,** and other departments to align security with business objectives and compliance requirements.
    - **Organization-wide responsibility:** Every employee, from front-line staff to executives, plays a role in maintaining operational security.
- **Testing Operational Security:**
    - Conducted by **internal security teams** or **external consultants** specializing in penetration testing and security assessments.
    - These tests identify **weaknesses** in the organization’s security posture before attackers can exploit them.
    - **Penetration testers** may:
        - Try to **bypass access controls**.
        - Exploit **system misconfigurations**.
        - Use **social engineering tactics** to evaluate real-world effectiveness of OpSec measures.
- **Goal:** Continuous improvement of operational defenses to ensure resilience against evolving threats.
