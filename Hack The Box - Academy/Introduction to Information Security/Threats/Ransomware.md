- **Ransomware** is a form of **malware** that encrypts files on computers, servers, or networks, making them **inaccessible** to the victim.
- **Attackers demand ransom**, typically in **cryptocurrency** (e.g., Bitcoin), in exchange for a **decryption key** to unlock the files.
- It functions like a **digital hostage situation**, where valuable data is taken captive until payment is made.
- Common **targets** include individuals, businesses, and critical infrastructure organizations.
- The goal is to **extort money** by leveraging the victim’s dependence on their data and operations.

- **Primary Purpose:** Ransomware aims for **financial gain** by extorting victims who need access to their encrypted data.
- **Targets:** Individuals, businesses, hospitals, and government organizations are common victims.
- **Impacts Beyond Money:**
    - **Operational disruption** — halts essential services or business functions.
    - **Reputational damage** — loss of trust among customers and partners.
    - **Public safety risks** — in sectors like healthcare, it can **endanger lives**.
- **Analogy — The Art Gallery:**
    - Your artwork is locked behind **impenetrable glass cases** overnight.
    - A ransom note demands payment for the **keys to unlock** your collection.
    - Until payment, your business cannot function — just like how **ransomware locks digital assets** until the ransom is paid.


# How it works
- **WannaCry Attack (May 2017):**
    - A global **ransomware outbreak** affecting **over 200,000 computers** across **150+ countries**.
    - Severely impacted the **UK’s National Health Service (NHS)** — hospitals lost access to patient data, surgeries were canceled, and ambulances were diverted.
    - Attackers demanded **$300–$600 in Bitcoin** for decryption.
    - Exploited a known **Microsoft Windows vulnerability** in systems lacking security updates.
    - Estimated **global damages reached billions of dollars**, exposing weaknesses in critical infrastructure.
- **Stages of a Ransomware Attack:**
    1. **Initial Access:**
        - Attackers infiltrate systems via **phishing emails** with malicious attachments or links.
    2. **Installation:**
        - Ransomware is **silently installed** on the victim’s computer.
    3. **Encryption:**
        - Files (documents, photos, databases) are encrypted with **complex algorithms**, making them unreadable.
    4. **Ransom Demand:**
        - A **ransom note** appears with payment instructions and a deadline to avoid permanent data loss.
    5. **Outcome:**
        - Even if the ransom is paid, attackers **may not provide** a valid decryption key.
        - Paying often **encourages further targeting** and fuels future cyberattacks.
- **Key Takeaways:**
    - Ransomware is a **multi-stage, financially motivated** form of cyber extortion.
    - **Unpatched systems** and **poor security hygiene** significantly increase vulnerability.
    - The **WannaCry incident** underscored the importance of **timely software updates**, **network segmentation**, and **robust backup strategies** to mitigate ransomware risks.


# Impact
- **Ransomware Threat Overview:**
    - A major cybersecurity threat capable of **widespread disruption** across industries.
    - Impacts extend far beyond the ransom itself, affecting **operations, finances, data integrity, and reputation**.
- **Key Impacts:**
    - **Operational Shutdowns:**
        - Can halt entire business operations or critical services (e.g., hospitals unable to access patient data).
        - In sectors like healthcare or energy, disruptions can **endanger lives** or public safety.
    - **Financial Losses:**
        - Include ransom payments, **downtime costs**, recovery efforts, and investments in new defenses.
    - **Data Loss:**
        - Permanent loss of vital data if **backups are unavailable or compromised**.
    - **Reputational Damage:**
        - High-profile breaches reduce **customer trust** and can cause **long-term revenue decline**.
    - **Perpetuation of Cybercrime:**
        - Paying ransoms **incentivizes attackers** and may mark victims for **future targeting**.
- **Analogy — The Locked Library:**
    - A **public library’s books** are locked in coded cases, and a **ransom note** demands payment to unlock them.
    - The entire community—students, researchers, and the public—loses access to vital resources.
    - Similarly, ransomware affects not just the victim organization but **everyone relying on its services**.
- **Global Impact:**
    - **Cybercrime costs** are projected to reach **trillions of dollars annually by 2025** (Cybersecurity Ventures).
    - Ransomware remains one of the **most financially and operationally devastating** attack types worldwide.
