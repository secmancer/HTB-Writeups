## 1. Network Foundations
- Definition of networking: short for computer networking
- Why networking matters in modern society: We use computer networking to communicate with each other from all parts of the globe
- Relevance to cybersecurity: networking is a fundamental part of cybersecurity that you should know about
- Interconnected devices and security demand: almost every device we have is connected to a network in some way, and that device should be secured
---
## 2. What is a Network?
### Definition
### Core Components
- **Nodes:** the individual devices that are connected to a network
- **Links:** the communication links between the nodes, can be either wired or wireless
- **Data Sharing:** the primary purpose of a network, in which we need to share data between each other
### Examples of Nodes
- smartphones, computers, printers, servers, etc.
### Real-World Analogy (optional)
- When we are in a group talking, we can represent ourselves as a network:
	- Person (node)
	- Talking/listening (links)
	- Conversation (data sharing)
---
## 3. Why Networks Are Important
### Resource Sharing
- multiple devices can often share both hardware and software resources
### Communication
- services like email, video calls, and instant messaging need networking to run
### Data Access
- connected devices can be able to access/share files and databases
### Collaboration
- Devices are able to easily work together in real time with no issues, even if placed miles and miles away
---
## 4. Types of Networks
- ## Networks vary by:
    - size and scope
### Main Categories
- Local Area Network (LAN)
- Wide Area Network (WAN)
---
## 5. Local Area Network (LAN)
### Definition
- Connecting devices over a short distance, like within the same home, school, or building
### Characteristics
- **Geographical Scope:**
    - small
- **Ownership:**
    - owned/managed by either a single person or organization
- **Speed:**
    - high data transfer rates
- **Media (Wired/Wireless):**
    - Can use either wired or wireless communication
---
## 6. Wide Area Network (WAN)

### Definition
- a group of multiple LANs connected together
### Characteristics
- **Geographical Scope:**
	- large, can be cities, countries, or continents
- **Ownership:**
    - Often a collected/distributed partnership
- **Speed:**
	- slower transfer rates due to that long distance
- **Media:**
    - Uses faster means of connection, like fiber optics and telecommunication lines
### Example
- The Internet
---
## 7. LAN vs WAN Comparison
### Size
- LAN: Small
- WAN: Large
### Ownership
- LAN: Single individual/organization
- WAN: Multiple organizations
### Speed
- LAN: High
- WAN: Lower
### Maintenance
- LAN: Cheaper, easier
- WAN: Complex, costly
### Examples
- LAN: Home/office network
- WAN: The Internet
---
## 8. How LANs and WANs Work Together
### ISP (Internet Service Provider)
- Definition: company that provides individuals/organizations access to the internet
### Modem
- Meaning: (Modulator/Demodulator)
- Purpose: bridge your home network and the ISP infrastructure
- Signal conversion: converts the router's digital signals into a format that can be transmitted over various media, like fiber optics, cable systems, and telephone lines
